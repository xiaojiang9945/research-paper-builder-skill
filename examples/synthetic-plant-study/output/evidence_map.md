# Evidence Map

All evidence in this demo is synthetic.

## Study Question

Do the mock genotypes differ in seed germination retention under a salt treatment compared with water control?

## Study Design

| item | value |
|---|---|
| system | synthetic plant seed germination assay |
| genotypes | Mock-WT, Mock-Line-A, Mock-Line-B |
| treatments | Water, Salt |
| biological replicates | 3 per genotype x treatment |
| seeds per replicate | 25 |
| primary endpoint | Day 7 germination percentage |
| secondary endpoint | Day 3 germination percentage and Day 1-7 trajectory |

## Evidence Classes

| class | evidence | use in manuscript |
|---|---|---|
| measured result | daily germinated seed counts | Results and source data |
| derived statistic | germination percentage, mean, SD, salt retention | figure panels and summary table |
| interpretation | Mock-Line-A retains the highest Day 7 germination under salt | cautious Discussion claim |
| limitation | synthetic dataset, small replicate count, no mechanistic assay | limitation paragraph |

## Supported Result Themes

1. Water-treated seeds germinated strongly across all mock genotypes.
2. Salt treatment reduced Day 7 germination in every mock genotype.
3. Mock-Line-A showed the highest synthetic salt-treatment Day 7 germination and retention.
4. The dataset supports a descriptive tolerance-ranking example only; it does not support mechanism.

## Claims To Avoid

- Do not claim a real biological discovery.
- Do not claim salt-tolerance mechanism.
- Do not claim field performance, seed vigor, or species-level generality.
- Do not claim statistical significance beyond the provided descriptive summary.
- Do not claim that the synthetic citation set is a real literature review.
- Do not claim that the demo completed the 200-paper literature-intake gate required for a real high-ambition manuscript.
