# A synthetic seed-germination dataset demonstrates a figure-led manuscript workflow

## Abstract

Synthetic examples are useful for testing manuscript-building workflows without exposing unpublished data. Here, we created a fictional seed-germination dataset containing three mock plant genotypes, two treatments and three biological replicates per genotype-treatment combination. Water-treated seeds reached high Day 7 germination across genotypes, with means from 94.7% to 98.7%. Salt treatment reduced Day 7 germination in all mock genotypes, but the synthetic Mock-Line-A group retained the highest salt-treated germination at 32.0%, compared with 17.3% for Mock-Line-B and 4.0% for Mock-WT. These synthetic results illustrate how an evidence map, terminology ledger, figure plan, manuscript draft and reviewer-risk audit can be assembled without using private research materials.

## Introduction

Research-writing workflows often fail when drafting begins before the evidence boundary is defined. This can lead to inconsistent terminology, unsupported claims, stale figure references and citation gaps. A safe demonstration requires data that are structured enough to support a complete manuscript package but are not derived from a real unpublished project.

Seed-germination assays provide a compact model for demonstrating this workflow because the input table, endpoint summary and figure source data can be inspected directly. In this synthetic example, three mock genotypes were compared under water and salt treatment. The goal was not to infer biology, but to show how a manuscript-building skill can convert a small dataset into traceable writing and submission-support materials.

## Results

### Water-treated seeds germinated strongly across mock genotypes

The water-treated groups reached high Day 7 germination in all three mock genotypes. Mean Day 7 germination was 97.3% for Mock-WT, 98.7% for Mock-Line-A and 94.7% for Mock-Line-B (Supplementary Table S1). This pattern provides a synthetic baseline in which the three mock genotypes have broadly similar germination under the control treatment.

### Salt treatment reduced Day 7 germination in every mock genotype

Salt treatment reduced the Day 7 endpoint in all mock genotypes (Figure 1a). The synthetic Mock-WT group decreased from 97.3% under water treatment to 4.0% under salt treatment. Mock-Line-A decreased from 98.7% to 32.0%, and Mock-Line-B decreased from 94.7% to 17.3%. Thus, the demonstration dataset supports a descriptive treatment effect in which salt reduces germination across the mock panel.

### Mock-Line-A had the highest synthetic salt-retention value

The salt-treatment time course separated the mock genotypes by Day 3 and remained stable through Day 7 (Figure 1b). At the Day 7 endpoint, salt-retention percentage was 32.4% for Mock-Line-A, 18.3% for Mock-Line-B and 4.1% for Mock-WT (Figure 1c). This ranking supports a cautious demonstration statement: in the synthetic dataset, Mock-Line-A retains the highest germination under salt treatment. It does not support a mechanistic claim or real genotype recommendation.

## Discussion

This synthetic example shows how a small dataset can be converted into a manuscript package while preserving claim discipline. The primary result is descriptive: salt treatment reduced germination in all mock genotypes, and Mock-Line-A showed the highest Day 7 salt-retention value. Because the data are fictional and limited to three replicates per group, no biological mechanism or field-level implication should be inferred.

The example also demonstrates why manuscript drafting should be linked to source data. The figure plan identifies the source data for each panel, the terminology ledger prevents inconsistent genotype naming, and the reviewer-risk audit records the limits that should not be hidden by polishing. In a real project, the same structure would help authors separate measured results, derived statistics, interpretation and limitations before preparing a submission package.

## Methods

The input dataset was manually constructed as a fully synthetic seed-germination table. Three mock genotypes were included: Mock-WT, Mock-Line-A and Mock-Line-B. Each genotype was represented under water and salt treatment with three biological replicates per treatment. Each replicate contained 25 seeds. Daily germination counts were recorded from Day 1 to Day 7.

Germination percentage was calculated as germinated seed count divided by 25 seeds. Salt-retention percentage was calculated as Day 7 mean germination under salt treatment divided by Day 7 mean germination under water treatment for the same genotype. Summary values are reported as mean +/- SD.
